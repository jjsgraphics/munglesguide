🥶 THE DOCTOR FREEZE ★★★★☆
Agent: OMEN  |  Type: Teleport Assassination  |  Discovered by: Mungle

The Doctor Freeze is a devastating Omen technique where you teleport directly behind an unsuspecting enemy and eliminate them before they can react. Named for the way your target freezes in confusion as they realise death came from behind.

How it's done:
  1. Identify an enemy holding an angle or peeking a sightline — they need to be focused on something ahead of them
  2. Use Omen's Shrouded Step (or From the Shadows ult for maximum disrespect) to teleport directly behind them
  3. While materialising, line up your crosshair on their back — they won't hear you in time
  4. One tap them from behind. They're frozen. They're finished. They're Doctor Freeze'd. 🥶
The Doctor Freeze works best when the enemy is scoped in or distracted by a teammate's push. The more focused they are, the more free the kill is.
Attempting the Doctor Freeze against a Cypher with trapwires behind them WILL get you caught. Check your corners before you TP.
👀 THE MUNG PEEK ★★★☆☆
Agent: ANY  |  Type: Jiggle Bait  |  Discovered by: Mungle

The Mung Peek is a universal technique where you jiggle your character model on a corner to bait the enemy into thinking you're about to peek — but you never actually commit. They shoot, they whiff, they overcommit... and then you punish.

How it's done:
  1. Position yourself on a corner where an enemy is likely holding an angle
  2. Rapidly tap your strafe key to jiggle your character model just barely past the edge — just enough for them to see a pixel of you
  3. IMMEDIATELY pull back. DO NOT commit to the peek. This is the crucial part — you are BAITING, not peeking.
  4. The enemy panics and fires, revealing their position. Now you know exactly where they are. Re-peek and delete them, or rotate and call it out.
The Mung Peek is extra effective at lower ranks where players have itchy trigger fingers. At higher ranks, combine it with a flash or smoke for maximum bamboozle.
👻 THE OMEN GUIDE ESSENTIAL
OMEN
CONTROLLER // SHADOW HUNTER

Omen is the teleport king of Valorant, and if you want to play like Mungle, you need to understand one fundamental truth:

TELEPORT. CONSTANTLY. ALWAYS BE TELEPORTING.

The #1 mistake new Omen players make is using their abilities "safely." Forget safe. Mungle Omen is about chaos. Your Shrouded Step isn't a repositioning tool — it's a weapon. Your ult isn't for flanking once a half — it's for psychological warfare.

MUNGLE'S OMEN COMMANDMENTS
  1. Shrouded Step aggressively. Behind the enemy, on top of boxes, onto elevated positions mid-fight. If you're not teleporting at least 3-4 times a half, you're playing Omen wrong.
  2. Smoke for yourself, not just the team. Throw a smoke, then TP INSIDE the smoke. Now you're invisible AND repositioned. The enemy has no idea where you went.
  3. Use the paranoia. The Dark Cover (smoke) + Paranoia (blind) + Shrouded Step combo is the holy trinity. Blind them, smoke it, TP behind, and Doctor Freeze.
  4. Your ult is a mind game. Sometimes you ult just to cancel it — making the enemy rotate for nothing. Sometimes you ult into their spawn just to assert dominance. There are no rules.
  5. Always. Be. Teleporting. The moment you stop moving, you become predictable. Predictable Omens are dead Omens. Mungle Omens are ghosts.

🎩 THE CHAMBER GUIDE SIMPLE
CHAMBER
SENTINEL // FRENCH MARKSMAN

Right, you want a Chamber guide? Here's the thing about Chamber:

SHOOT THE ENEMY.

That's it. That's the guide. Chamber is a point-and-click adventure game. You have a gun (Headhunter), an even bigger gun (Tour de Force), a teleport anchor to escape when things go bad, and a slow trap to help you hold sites.

But the core of Chamber? Aim. If you can aim, you're a good Chamber. If you can't aim, play someone else. Preferably Omen so you can do Doctor Freezes instead.

MUNGLE'S CHAMBER COMMANDMENTS
  1. Headhunter = Sheriff but better. Use it like a Sheriff. Aim for the head. If you hit the head, they die. Revolutionary stuff.
  2. Tour de Force = free Operator. Get kills with the ult. Every kill makes a slow field. More kills = more slow fields. It's not complicated.
  3. Place your teleport anchor. Stand somewhere aggressive, place your TP behind cover. Get a pick, TP out. Repeat until the enemy team surrenders.
  4. Trademark (trap) on flank. Put it where people sneak through. When it goes off, shoot the person. Simple.
  5. Literally just click on their heads. That's it. That's the guide. Go practice your aim.
Chamber's entire kit rewards mechanical skill. If you want a shortcut, there isn't one. Go to the range, practice headshots, and come back when you can one-tap.

🎯 CROSSHAIR SETTINGS META

You want to aim better? Forget those tiny little dot crosshairs the pros use. Mungle has perfected the ultimate crosshair — and the science behind it is undeniable.

"PHAT"
AKA "BIG BERTHA" 🫡
If you are missing your shots, there's really only one explanation: you can't see your crosshair. Think about it. You're in a firefight, muzzle flashes everywhere, smokes going off, your screen is shaking — and you're supposed to track a little 2-pixel dot? NAH.

The solution is simple. Make it BIGGER. Make it so big you can NEVER lose it. If your crosshair takes up a significant portion of the screen, you will ALWAYS know exactly where your bullets are going. That's just facts. That's just science. That's BIG BERTHA.

📜 The Hidden Lore

🧀 The Legend of Tim Cheese

Tim Cheese, born May 5th, 1962, in Chicago, Illinois, is a notorious criminal mastermind and leader of the Breadsticks gang. Abandoned as a child on the streets of O-Block, he grew up with no known parents — but scientists later confirmed he is the cousin of Biggie Cheese, the famous rapper.

From a young age, Tim was part of a motley crew: Tim Cheese, Simon Claw, and John Pork. These unruly youths were often seen playing on the streets, occasionally stealing luscious and rare strains of cheese — possibly causing Tim's passionate infatuation with exotic cheeses later in life.

Unlike most gangsters who relied on brute strength, Tim's power came from his cunning, manipulation, and tactical brilliance. He quickly rose through the ranks, founding the Breadsticks — a gang that would expand its influence across the United States and into Portugal and Ireland. He recruited his cousin, Justin Cheddar, as a trusted associate, further solidifying his power.

Throughout his career, Tim orchestrated several large-scale criminal operations: the Pacific Drug Smuggling scheme of 1986, the Sniffing Cheese Smuggling of 1992, and The Saga of 1997 — one of his most notorious crime waves. Adding to his near-mythical status, there are unconfirmed reports that Tim possesses the ability to morph from a 1ft, 23lb rat into a 6'8", 198lb human form.

In late 2024, Tim was captured and held in Area 51, but he managed to escape after six days by hijacking a military helicopter. On February 18, 2025, a team of six FBI agents tracked him down. Tim killed five of them and left one alive to deliver a chilling message:

"No one can stop me." — Tim Cheese, to the surviving FBI agent, 2025

🐷 The John Pork Saga

All was well in the trio until John Pork fell under the illusion that he was too superior for the group. Fuelled with arrogance and a dangerous obsession with Tina Cheese — Tim's girlfriend at the time — he shattered the once-inseparable trio. Simon Claw was quoted as saying:

"John Pork thought he could wipe off the mud and walk away like royalty." — Simon Claw

John leveraged his newfound fame to snatch Tina Cheese from Tim. This devastating betrayal marked the beginning of a dark path in Tim's life. The deep-seated rivalry runs back to their youth — Tim's bitter resentment and fury drove him to seek brutal revenge upon his former friend, culminating in John Pork's cold-blooded murder.

But the story doesn't end there. John Pork was brought back to life through the noble and valiant efforts of a mysterious figure. It is hotly disputed whether Adam Crow or Agent 5.5 revived Pork, but as quoted by the detective's report:

"It is unknown how he was able to revive Pork's lifeless body." — Detective's Report

Meanwhile, Simon Claw remained loyal to Tim throughout everything. Even after John abandoned them, Simon stayed and committed crimes alongside Tim. Eventually the two got cornered — Simon took the fall and saved Tim by letting him flee. Simon spent 5 years in prison on a life sentence, a testament to the unbreakable bond between the original Breadsticks.

The saga continues. Tim Cheese is still at large. John Pork walks among the living. And somewhere, Simon Claw waits.

"When John Pork calls, you answer. Not because you want to. Because you have to." — JP 3:16
